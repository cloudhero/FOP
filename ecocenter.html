Ecotourism
More than 55 million US travelers are considered ecotourists interested in nature, culture and heritage. According to the world tourism Association, ecotourism is one of the fastest growing travel trends and is estimated to be a $77 billion market.

The nonprofit (501 c 3) eco-center will be a unique ecotourists destination; drawing people from across the world that are concerned with ecological stewardship. More than 30 acres of tentacle gardens and green space along with natural and constructed wetlands, provide a parklike setting and encourage self guided horticulture and bird watching tours.

Visitors can explore a working greenhouse and outdoor nurseries as well as observe firsthand the centers eco-village. Developers, community leaders, citizens and students can witness the benefits of capturing treated wastewater and storm water and using it as an asset rather than liability. Scientific exploration trips to the bayou and local marine sanctuaries will introduce the world to the natural habitat and unique ecology.

    Water Supply
    Capable of accepting water from any source during a disaster, the water filtration and purification plant will demonstrate water production using a simple modular process and components that can be assembled in short notice. The system is energy-efficient, and also includes proprietary equipment for softening, improving irrigation efficiency, cooling system performance, and crop yields.

    Aquaponics
    Aquaponics refers to the combined production flora and fauna in a macrocosm that recirculate the water used to raise fish, crawfish, and shrimp to aquatic farming production stages that produce crops, such as lettuce and basil. The nutrient waste from the aquaculture seafood serves as fertilizer for the plant production, which in turn cleans and circulates the water.

    Anoxic gasification is a scalable process that heats and vaporizes a wide range of wastes to produce a syngas that can then be used to directly produce electricity, or converted to liquid fuels, including bio diesel, ethanol, methanol.

    Desalination
    The EcoCenter will demonstrate state-of-the-art desalination approaches that address the two main factors that inhibit wider deployment of membrane technology: energy costs, and membrane life. The technology includes proprietary devices in unique combinations of readily available equipment.

    Low Impact Development
    In an area so dramatically affected by flooding, The EcoCenter will employ all of the tools of LID to demonstrate watershed restoration, even in an urbanized setting. This shall include stream drainage restoration, wetlands and buffer restoration, groundwater and base flow recharge, previous paving systems, runoff capture and reuse, environmental restoration using recycled water, and an overall balancing of previous and impervious areas across the project site.
