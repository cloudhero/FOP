  6. The USBF™ Bioreactor Process
    As it rises, upward velocity decreases until the flocs of cells become stationary, forming a boundary layer and acting as a living, dynamic efficient filtering media, and producing clear, highly-treated effluent. Filtered effluent overflows via a collection weir and is steadily discharged from the system. Heavier flocs descend to the bottom of the sludge filter, and subsequently are recycled back into the anoxic zone.
