USBF Frequently Asked Questions

How does USBF® handle fluctuating inflows?
Answer: The sludge filter's prism or cone shape facilitates superior hydraulic flexibility. It accommodates high peak flows and flow swings in a self-regulating manner; the higher the flow, the higher the sludge blanket rises and the larger the filtration area becomes.


How does the USBF® process prevent odor?
Answer: Most odors associated with collection systems and primary treatment facilities are generated as a result of an anaerobic or "septic" condition. USBF avoids and anaerobic stage with aerobic conditions throughout the bioreactor and extended sludge age to eliminate or dramatically reduce the potential for bioreactor originated odor.


How does the USBF® process result in reduced operating and maintenance requirements?
Answer: Compact design, minimal amount of moving parts, modularity of construction and self-regulating hydraulics result in reduced supervision requirements, and operating and maintenance costs. With our one tank design, the need to pump water from tank to tank is eliminated with a corresponding reduction in electricity and complexity.


Is the one tank design part of your promise of a smaller footprint for the treatment facility?
Answer: Yes, the USBF® process incorporates nitrification, denitrification, clarification and sludge stabilization in one compact bioreactor which reduces equipment size and liquid handling requirements and ultimately leads to a smaller plant footprint.


Is it true that USBF® systems do not require a primary clarification stage?
Answer: Yes, the USBF® technology does not require primary clarification prior to biological treatment. Adequate screening facility and for larger plants, grit removal system are all that is required upstream of the bioreactors.


How does the USBF® include Biological Nutrient Removal?
Answer: Denitrification (dissimilarity nitrate reduction) occurs in the anoxic compartment and phosphorus removal happens as the liquor passes through the sludge blanket.


Does the USBF® System prevent excess alkalinity and provide bacteria control?
Answer: Yes, the integral denitrification process facilitates partial recovery of alkalinity loss during nitrification, and the anoxic compartment serves as a “selector zone” that conditions the mixed liquor to improve settleability and to control filamentous organism growth.
